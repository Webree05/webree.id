Inside Our Process

Inside Our Indonesian
Charcoal Production

Experience the journey of authentic Indonesian hardwood turning into premium charcoal. From sustainable timber harvesting to traditional mud kiln baking and meticulous manual sorting, we ensure every piece meets global standards.

01. Premium Hardwood Selection

The foundation of premium charcoal is the wood it comes from. We harvest high-density Indonesian hardwoods, such as Halaban and Tamarind, from sustainable sources. The logs are cut into proper sizes and stacked, ready to be transformed. The density of these specific woods guarantees a longer burn time and consistent, high heat for the final product.

  • High-density hardwood (Halaban, Tamarind)
  • Sustainably sourced timber
  • Optimally sized for even carbonization

02. Preparation to the Kilns

The selected logs are manually transported using traditional wooden carts across the production grounds. This manual handling ensures that the wood is inspected one last time before entering the carbonization phase. The logs are then skillfully arranged inside the kilns to maximize airflow and ensure an even, perfect burn.

03. The Art of Carbonization

We utilize traditional dome-shaped mud kilns to bake the wood. These kilns, crafted from specialized clay, are masters at trapping heat while slowly burning off moisture and impurities over several weeks. This age-old oxygen-restricted baking method creates charcoal that burns hotter, longer, and cleaner without chemical accelerants.

"The mud kilns are the heart of our production. They breathe and hold the heat exactly as the hardwood requires to turn into pure carbon."

04. Harvesting & Manual Sorting

Once the kilns have cooled, our experienced workers enter the mud domes to harvest the pristine black carbon. Every single piece is manually sorted and graded. We sift out dust and separate the pieces by size to meet export-grade requirements for BBQ, restaurants, and industrial use globally.
